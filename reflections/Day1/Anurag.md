**When I first saw the problem statement, I thought the real task was...**

To help students actually learn in the age of AI. LLMs just give you the full answer to any doubt you have but they never question you back or test if you really understood something. Real learning needs that socratic push, not just an output.

**The first thing I personally wanted to do was...**

Come up with a solid idea after understanding the problem. I thought of a gamified platform with levels and mini games, where each game teaches a different aspect of learning with AI. Like one game could show two AI responses and ask the student to spot which one is hallucinating.

**The first thing our team actually did was...**

Brainstorm and read research papers to understand the problem better. We made a Google Doc to put all our ideas and thoughts in one place.

**That helped because...**

We got to see everyone's perspective and understand the problem from different angles. It helped us move toward a more concrete idea.

**That hurt because...**

I don't really think there was much to be hurt here. Coming up with a lot of ideas and narrowing them down based on experimentation and resources is just part of the process. The difficulty in converging was development happening, not a setback.

**One assumption I made about teenagers, learning or AI was...**

Based on my own learning I assumed questioning your beliefs is what builds real understanding. So I assumed our multi agent system would naturally push students in that direction and behave exactly the way I had imagined it would.
