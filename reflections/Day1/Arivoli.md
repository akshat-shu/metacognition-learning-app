**When I first saw the problem statement, I thought the real task was...**

I thought the task was to build an app or tool for teenagers to learn to grow up using AI with the awareness of how it could affect their mental ability if they trust it blindly for cognitive tasks.

**The first thing I personally wanted to do was...**

I wanted to build an app that would teach a topic step by step and ask questions at every step, and then have a quiz at the end. But apparently that already exists with Gemini guided learning so we discussed other ideas like a gamified learning app and a teacher-like app and so on. 

**The first thing our team actually did was...**

A google document to share and unify all our ideas in one place so that everyone can read and ideate on what the others are thinking and upto. I shared a few research papers there, and anurag stumbled upon the same paper on his own, so we started working on that paper's ideas. 

**That helped because...**

Our whole team now had an idea of what everyone was upto, and different perspectives added up, and helped us converge on one final idea. 

**That hurt because...**

I suppose I had to read a lot of text but that's about it haha. It did more help being organized than any hurt I believe. It was a necessary step. 

**One assumption I made about teenagers, learning or AI was...**

I had this assumption that teenagers if they are aware, they will use ai wisely on their own and that this app was not that necessary. But this was shutdown very quickly, and looking back, that was not a great assumption from my end. Young students do not need to have the maturity to make that decision, and I realized it was hypocritical of me to assume that since I've made that mistake myself at different points.
